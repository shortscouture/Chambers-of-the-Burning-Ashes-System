St. Gregory of Narek (c. 1010) was an Armenian monk, poet, theologian, and philosopher, honored as a saint in both the Armenian Apostolic and Catholic Churches. Born into a family of writers, he spent his life in the monastery of Narek (Narekavank), where he became “Armenia’s first great poet” and was revered as “the watchful angel in human form.” His most famous work, Book of Lamentations, is a masterpiece of poetic devotion and deep theological reflection. Recognized as a Doctor of the Church, Gregory’s writings continue to inspire spiritual healing and a profound connection with God. His legacy of faith and humility endures, offering hope and renewal to all who seek it.

The Feast of the Chair of St. Peter celebrates the unique role entrusted to Peter by Christ as the leader of the apostles and the Church. More than a physical object, the Chair symbolizes the Pope’s mission to guide, teach, and unify the faithful across the world. This feast reminds us of the enduring promise of Christ to be with His Church, and the importance of unity under the guidance of Peter’s successors. Let us pray for the Pope and embrace the call to build a Church rooted in love, service, and truth.

✖

St. Peter Damian, a 12th-century monk and cardinal, was known for his deep humility, tireless dedication to Church reform, and theological brilliance. Despite his quiet life as a hermit, his writings and actions addressed corruption and sought to renew the faith of the clergy and laity. Peter’s life reminds us that reform begins within. His pursuit of holiness and his love for Christ made him a powerful voice for truth and integrity. May his example inspire us to live faithfully and serve with courage
